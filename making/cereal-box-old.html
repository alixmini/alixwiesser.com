cereal box gift

For the month of July in 2024, I dabbled in the basics of UX Design while making my way through the murky waters of possible career trajectories. This involved a Figma crash course and tracing a lot of existing UIs to familiarise myself with alignment and making the little icons from scratch. It was fun! I ultimately decided UX Design wasn’t for me, but I retained my love for Figma.

Let’s tie this to the cereal box project, which started its life as a gift for a special person I was visiting. When I was younger, I wasn’t allowed to read or be on a screen while eating breakfast before school, so I’d often read the milk carton or the cereal box. The crosswords and other games were especially fun. Fast forward to 2022, when I went to the USA for the first time and discovered Trader Joe’s — imagine my delight. The packaging was wonderful and whimsical. This would serve as design inspiration for the cereal box. I had a pretty clear vision of how I wanted the box to look: retro, colourful, legit. I started by unfolding an empty cardboard cereal box from my cupboard and tracing the template.
