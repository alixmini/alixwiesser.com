POSTERS

Everyone wants posters until they start looking for posters. I want my walls to be filled with things I love! My walls should be a reflection of my me-ness! Redbubble will not do. Amazon certainly will not. My pockets are not nearly deep enough for the most epic posters. I wanted my walls to feel like home, though - this is still a work in progress overall in our flat, to be fair. There's a lot of white space waiting to be filled. Anyway!

In classic TCK fashion, I grew up and have called several places home. London is now my primary home, and the second is wherever my parents are living - currently and for the foreseeable, that's France. With this in mind, I started looking for a series of posters of these different places. Ultimately, because I wanted them to look cohesive and it's a bit of a mish-mash of places, this had to be a DIY project.

The general vibe of the posters emerged over time: Colourful, interpretative (???), somewhat juvenile in style, and a nice gift to share copies of with friends who lived in those places with you. I traced a lot of landmarks and doodling on bits that felt more specific to my memories of these places. Sketchbook is a great, free iPad app for art, especially if you don't need the fanciest tools, which I didn't and don't.

When I first put these up, I was living with my brother, so I also did a little Loughborough poster for him, with a football field and pints. I wasn't sure what else to put. When Sasha moved out and my friend Elliot moved in (yay!), I added an Exeter poster to the mix and filmed the process.

I had them printed in A3 in 120gsm, usually at a Ryman store. When I one day move out of this flat, I'll likely have to cough up some repair fees for the off-brand glue dots I used to stick them up, but that's a problem for future Mini!
