CEREAL BOX

For the month of July in 2024, I dabbled in the basics of UX Design, while making my way through the murky waters of possible career trajectories. This involved a Figma crash course and tracing a lot of existing UIs to familiarise myself with alignment and making the little icons from scratch. It was fun! I ultimately decided UX Design wasn't for me, but I retained my love for Figma.

Let's tie this to the cereal box project, which started its life as a gift for a friend I was visiting.

When I was younger, I wasn't allowed to read or be on a screen while eating breakfast before school, so I'd often read the milk carton or the cereal box. The crosswords and other games were especially fun. Fast forward to 2022, when I went to the USA for the first time and discovered Trader Joe's! Imagine my delight. The packaging was wonderful and whimsical. This would serve as design inspiration for the cereal box.

Okay, so cereal box, fun packaging, newfound design skills. I had a pretty clear vision of how I wanted the box to look: retro, colourful, legit. I started by unfolding an empty cardboard cereal box from my cupboard and recreated each crease and fold as a template. For the crossword and riddle on the back, I used online generators and AI to speed up the process.

To this day, this is one of my favourite things I've made. I wanted to share that gift with people, so I made a template and cleaned up the Figma file so you can make your own! Admittedly, it's not the mooost user-friendly tutorial, but my friend Erica made one for her friend, so it's definitely doable!
