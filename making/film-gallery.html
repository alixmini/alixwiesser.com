Film gallery

I got into film photography after umming and ahing over the cost for ages. I was a big follower of the disposable Kodak camera trend as a teenager but felt intimidated by the price and learning curve of 'real' film. I bought my first camera at Clock Tower Cameras in Brighton on my twenty‑third birthday, a little Pentax Espio for sixty‑odd sterling. It’s a 35mm point‑and‑shoot and I adore it. Writing this has made me realise I haven’t named her, so I’m taking ten seconds to stare out the window and christen her. Okay, nice — we’re going with Margot. Margot and I go through a roll of 36 photos in about 5 or 6 weeks on average, and get the negatives developed at Aperture Printing on Rathbone Place. Richard, who is always there when I go, is patient and discerning; I think they do something special in their lab to make the quality come out so beautifully.
