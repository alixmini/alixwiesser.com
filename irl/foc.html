Feeling of Computing (previously known as Future of Coding) is a recurring London event spearheaded by Maggie Appleton of digital garden fame, among other great ideas and ways of thinking.

The awesome thing about FOC as an event isn't so much that it's built around coding, in my opinion, but more so the collective attitude toward exploring niche and oft-whimsical ideas. People demo side projects and present their ideas in a way that feels set apart from SV/VC hype. It's pure fun.

I spoke about personal websites - why we should all have our own; what I decided to include in mine (the legacy one, not this current one you're reading!); and some of my favourite examples.

TL;DR: [yourname].com should be a fun, self-indulgent CV that pulls people into your orbit. Most of us can afford to be a little less quiet about ourselves. Surprisingly (or not?), you can also make friends by being public about what makes you tick rather than what makes you impressive (see: LinkedIn).
