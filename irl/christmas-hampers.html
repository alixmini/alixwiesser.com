XMAS HAMPERS

December, 2025. A new term: "grandma-maxxing," coined by (afaik) yours truly. In an effort to be more neighbourly, especially as the only 20-somethings living in the building, I made some festive goodie bags for my neighbours and coworkers.

I'm going to list out the process in case you'd like to do something similar!

  1. Come up with a menu, mostly non- or semi-perishables. I went with (after consulting Claude):
    • Pear & cardamom shrub for festive drinks
    • Coconut, cashew, and chocolate granola
    • A jar of pickles (cauliflower, carrot, radish, cucumbers, big fat clove of garlic)
    • Pannetone(ish) biscotti (orange zest glaze!)
  2. Acquire ingredients
  3. Acquire vessels & packaging (ribbons, paper bags, stickers for labelling)
  4. Put on your apron and Christmas music. The making of everything took about 4 hours? Not bad!
  5. Design menu card, write a personal message on the back. Print lots of (well, enough) copies!
  6. Package everything!
  7. Hand-deliver your goods. Congratulations, you are now a grandma!

Keep recipes on hand or ask Claude to create an approximation of the ingredients you eyeballed, especially for the granola, if you make granola. People will want more of the granola.

Happy holidays! :))
