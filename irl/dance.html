DANCE

Dancing was a big deal when I was in school. We had an annual United Nations dance showcase, with student-choreographed and -organised dances from different cultures. The parents would argue in the group chats every year about whether Bhangra or Korean Drumming should be the finale, which was kind of fair enough because they were both incredible spectacles, although it might have been a bit overkill.

I'd never danced before, but I did fall in love with it. Most of us who participated had never had any formal dance training but one of the great things about humans is that we're made to move. We'd head to the dance studios at 3PM and on the weekends to learn the choreo, the ever-changing formations, and argue about costumes for the final performances. Teachers were minimally involved save for the occasional firm comment about keeping things family-friendly.

At uni, I joined the dance team, taught a beginners' heels class once a week, and also joined a dance group during my semester at Berkeley (see above). Performing is thrilling, but the most fun is always had during rehearsals.

I dance occasionally in London, and on top of trying to go a few times a month, I'm also trying to find a balance between beginner and intermediate classes (wherein a lot of professional dancers attend to sharpen their technique) because I land somewhere in the middle of the two. For those looking for a studio, Pineapple and The Manor are excellent!
