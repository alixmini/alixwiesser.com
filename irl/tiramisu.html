THE NEXT TIRAMISU CONTEST

My friend Ben James sent me a link to Dessert Quest and said I should enter, so I did. I thought it would be a chill dozen-person event, but it was not!

The challenge was to create The Next Tiramisu, which meant it was of utmost importance that I enlisted my friend Becca to help me, as she’s the queen of beautiful baking. We spent about two hours total on ideation before settling on a cake.

We also vibecoded a dessert idea generator by inputting all possible flavours, textures, and components that we might want to use, then jumbling them up. Ultimately, we didn’t like any of the combinations that Opus came up with, but it was a funny exercise.

Sadly, Becca wasn’t available to help with the bake on the day itself because she’s a professional pastry chef, but I gave it my best shot and put in a solid shift in the kitchen.

We created a:

  • Black sesame chiffon-esque sponge (which was supposed to be made in a rice cooker but there was too much batter so I popped it in the oven instead)
  • With a ginger-rosewater syrup
  • Frosted with a white chocolate & coconut ganache
  • Finished with a cornflake-sesame brittle

Honestly, delicious, but it didn’t quite survive the heat of the Victoria line in winter. The event was packed with at least sixteen contestants who all made gorgeous desserts. Everybody gave a speech, and the judges were very impressive (one of them had worked for Heston Blumenthal for a decade) which was mildly terrifying.

While they were deciding on the winners, we went around with plates and forks and tasted everything. I call this my definition of a perfect night (and perfect dinner). Also, lots of cool people.
