CNY OF THE SNAKE 2025

From kindergarten through to high school, we would have a dress-up day for Chinese New Year. Being half-Chinese, we would also typically celebrate at home and sometimes at friends' parties.

After university, my brother and I lived together in London for about a year, which was awesome. A lot of our friends from high school (I want to say at least 60% of my graduating class, but I could just be making that up) in Singapore also now live in London, and we all miss Din Tai Fung (the London branches suffer from shrinkflation). It just felt right to bring a bit of home into our new home.

Enter 2025, the Year Of The Snake! I sent out a Partiful, I put in a massive grocery order, I enslaved two of my best friends and we diced vegetables for the dumplings for three hours the night before the gathering.

On the day itself, a Saturday, most everybody showed up in red and was jolly. There were Pocky aplenty, a ripe pomelo was pulled apart, and my brother's British friends thought the mango-sago-pomelo dessert was a fish stew. A neighbour brought their baby, who was fawned over as is customary.

However, a word of warning: when you're a busy host intent on keeping people well-fed and -watered, people will offer help by way of topping up your prosecco, which is very kind and lovely. Please be warned, though, that you may very well head to the pub in the evening and end up with a sprained ankle. Take it from me, who concluded her CNY 2025 happy but hobbling.

Your 20s are for making your own traditions! It's all very exciting.
